Latin
During the year I have reviewed the use of verbs in the present tense and the use of commands. I have learned the possessive forms of nouns (genitive case). I am currently learning more about the use of the ablative case form of nouns (i.e. prepositional phrases, time phrases, and instrumental phrases). Soon I will be learning about the imperfect (past) tense of verbs. I have also learned that in Latin syntax (word order) is not important, but the endings on the ends of words are important. If a word is the subject of a Latin sentence, it has certain ending; and if the word is the direct object of a sentence, it has a different ending. Verbs in Latin also have endings which indicate who is doing the action. I am also learning how to recognize English words that come from Latin words. I investigated the tradition of naming in Roman society and compared the ancient Roman system to our system of naming.
